# Guide to Key Agricultural Schemes for Kerala Farmers (2025)

This document outlines major government schemes available to support farmers in Kerala. Farmers are encouraged to visit their local Krishi Bhavan for applications and detailed guidelines.

## 1. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
- **Objective:** To provide income support to all eligible small and marginal farmer families across the country.
- **Benefits:**
  - Financial assistance of ₹6,000 per year, transferred directly to the farmer's bank account in three equal installments of ₹2,000.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Farmer families (husband, wife, and minor children) who collectively own up to 2 hectares of cultivable land.
  - Must have a valid Aadhaar card and a bank account.
- **How to Apply:**
  - Through the official PM-KISAN portal or by visiting the local Krishi Bhavan or Akshaya Centre.

## 2. Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)
- **Objective:** To incentivize states to increase public investment in Agriculture and allied sectors. In Kerala, this is used to fund various projects based on local needs.
- **Benefits (Examples of sub-projects in Kerala):**
  - **Precision Farming:** Subsidy for setting up polyhouses, drip irrigation systems, and fertigation units. Up to 50% subsidy on the total cost.
  - **Organic Farming Support:** Financial assistance for organic certification, production of organic manure, and setting up vermicompost units.
  - **Farm Mechanization:** Subsidy on the purchase of agricultural machinery like tractors, tillers, and transplanters to promote mechanization.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Varies by sub-project. Generally available to individual farmers, Self Help Groups (SHGs), and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs).
- **How to Apply:**
  - Proposals are usually invited by the Department of Agriculture through Krishi Bhavans at the start of the financial year.

## 3. Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM)
- **Objective:** To increase the reach of farm mechanization to small and marginal farmers and to the regions where availability of farm power is low.
- **Benefits:**
  - Provides a subsidy of 40% to 50% for the purchase of a wide range of agricultural equipment.
  - Establishes Custom Hiring Centers (CHCs) where farmers can rent expensive machinery.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Individual farmers are eligible for subsidies on specific machines.
  - FPOs and SHGs are encouraged to set up CHCs with higher subsidies (up to 80% in some cases).
- **How to Apply:**
  - Applications are processed through the state's agriculture department portal.

## 4. Kerala State Crop Insurance Scheme
- **Objective:** To provide insurance coverage and financial support to farmers in the event of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases.
- **Benefits:**
  - Compensates farmers for losses based on the sum insured for a particular crop.
  - Low premium rates for farmers, with the government subsidizing a major portion.
- **Eligibility:**
  - All farmers (loanee and non-loanee) growing notified crops in notified areas are eligible.
  - It is mandatory for farmers who have taken agricultural loans from financial institutions.
- **How to Apply:**
  - Through the national crop insurance portal or designated banks and insurance companies. The enrollment period is usually before the start of the cropping season.
