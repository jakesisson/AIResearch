# Kerala Crop Calendar and Sowing Guide

This document provides a generalized crop calendar for major crops in Kerala. Timings can vary based on local monsoon patterns and specific microclimates.

## Kharif Season (South-West Monsoon: June - September)

This is the primary cropping season in Kerala, heavily reliant on the monsoon rains.

| Crop           | Variety Suggestions     | Sowing/Planting Period | Key Districts      | Notes                                                              |
|----------------|-------------------------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Rice (Paddy)** | Uma, Jyothi, Kanchana   | May - June             | Palakkad, Alappuzha, Kottayam | First crop, known as 'Virippu'. Transplanting should be done by mid-June. |
| **Banana**       | Nendran, Robusta        | April - May (pre-monsoon) | Thrissur, Ernakulam, Malappuram | Planting before the monsoon helps in root establishment. |
| **Ginger**       | Rio de Janeiro, Maran   | April - May            | Wayanad, Idukki    | Requires well-drained soil. Mulching is critical.                  |
| **Turmeric**     | Alleppey Supreme, Suguna | April - May            | Wayanad, Palakkad  | Similar requirements to Ginger. Inter-cropping is common.          |
| **Tapioca**      | H-165, Sree Jaya        | May - June             | Kollam, Thiruvananthapuram | Can tolerate a wide range of soil types.                           |
| **Brinjal**      | Haritha, Neelima        | May - June             | All districts      | Nursery should be raised in April.                                 |
| **Tomato**       | Sakthi, Mukthi          | May - June             | Idukki, Palakkad   | Staking is required for better yield.                              |

## Rabi Season (North-East Monsoon: October - January)

Also known as the second crop season or 'Mundakan' for rice.

| Crop           | Variety Suggestions     | Sowing/Planting Period | Key Districts      | Notes                                                              |
|----------------|-------------------------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Rice (Paddy)** | Ponni, Jaya, Pavizham   | September - October    | Palakkad, Alappuzha, Thrissur | Second crop. Water management is crucial as monsoon recedes.       |
| **Pulses (Cowpea)** | Kanakamony, V-16     | September - October    | Palakkad, Kollam   | Often grown in paddy fallows, utilizing residual moisture.         |
| **Sesame**       | Kayamkulam-1, Thilak    | September - October    | Alappuzha, Kollam  | Suitable for sandy loam soils.                                     |
| **Cucumber**     | Soubhagya, Arunima      | September - October    | All districts      | Can be grown in open fields and protected structures.              |
| **Bitter Gourd** | Priya, Preethi          | September - October    | All districts      | Requires a trellis for climbing.                                   |

## Summer Season (February - May)

This season relies on irrigation. It's the third crop season for rice, known as 'Puncha'.

| Crop           | Variety Suggestions     | Sowing/Planting Period | Key Districts      | Notes                                                              |
|----------------|-------------------------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Rice (Paddy)** | Kuttanadan Puncha varieties | December - January     | Kuttanad region (Alappuzha, Kottayam) | Special cultivation below sea level. Highly dependent on water levels. |
| **Watermelon**   | Sugar Baby, Charleston Gray | January - February     | Palakkad, Thrissur | Requires sandy soil and consistent irrigation.                     |
| **Okra (Lady's Finger)** | Salkeerthi, Aruna | January - February     | All districts      | High-yielding crop with good market demand.                        |
| **Amaranthus**   | CO-1, Kannara Local     | Throughout the year, peak in Jan-Feb | All districts      | Leafy vegetable, quick growing cycle.                              |
